Homunculus: A cortical homunculus is a physical representation of the human body, located within the brain. A cortical homunculus is a neurological "map" of the anatomical divisions of the body. There are two types of cortical homunculi: sensory and motor.
James-Lange Theory of Emotion: emotion is then experienced when the brain reacts to the information received via the body's nervous system.
Astroglia: star-shaped glial cell
Microglia: Microglia are a type of glial cell located throughout the brain and spinal cord.
Oligodendroglia: another type of glial cell
Cerebral Commissures: commissure refers to a bundle of nerve fibers that cross the midline at their level of origin or entry. Periaqueductal Gray (PAG): the grey matter located around the cerebral aqueduct within the tegmentum of the midbrain. primary control center for descending pain modulation with enkephalin-producing cells that suppress pain
